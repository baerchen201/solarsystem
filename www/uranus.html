Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and is distinguished by its striking blue-green color, caused by methane in its atmosphere. It is an ice giant with a diameter of 50,724 kilometers, making it the third-largest planet in the solar system. Unlike most planets, Uranus rotates on its side, giving it a unique axial tilt. Its atmosphere is primarily composed of hydrogen, helium, and methane, and it is surrounded by a faint system of rings made of dark particles and small moons.
